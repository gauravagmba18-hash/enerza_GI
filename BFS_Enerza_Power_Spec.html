Key Design Decisions
Expert developer choices vs SAP IS-U equivalents
Business Partner hub (not Customer)
Mirrors SAP IS-U: single BP can hold multiple contract accounts across gas+power+water — essential for convergent billing and BP-level credit scoring
Contract Account separates financial from physical
Billing address / dunning / payment method live on CA; physical details live on Installation — enables split billing for multi-premise customers
Point of Delivery (POD) as universal ID
Every installation gets a POD — mandatory for deregulated market readiness (CERC open access), meter data exchange (MDMS), and net metering tracking
FI-CA Open Item subledger (not direct accounting)
All financial postings go through FI_CA_OPEN_ITEM before GL — enables dunning, installment plans, and dispute holds without touching accounting entries
Interval Data as partitioned BIGINT PK table
15-min reads at 200K smart meters = 3.8 billion rows/year. Partition by month + meter range. Never use UUID for this table — sequential BIGINT only
Effective-date governance on all master data
Every master record has effective_from + effective_to. No hard deletes. Tariff changes, category changes, load changes — all tracked temporally for audit and rebilling
FEEDER_ENERGY_ACCOUNT for AT&C loss
Monthly energy balance per feeder enables AT&C loss computation without a separate DMS — keeps loss analytics inside the billing DB without a separate SCADA integration
Separate SMART_METER table extending METER
Not all meters are smart — legacy electromechanical coexists with AMI. SMART_METER extends METER only for AMI devices, keeping base table clean

Rate Plan & Tariff Management
Tariff
Rate plan master: consumer category, voltage level, SERC order reference, effective dates
Rate steps (slabs): sequence-ordered, open-ended top slab (to_units=NULL)
Charge components: meter rent, fixed charge, demand charge, taxes, duties

📄
Bill Computation Engine
Billing
Energy charge: slab-rated kWh consumption with multiplier applied
Fixed charge: meter rent (phase-wise), minimum guarantee charge
GST: 18% on meter rent; electricity duty: 15% on energy charges
Demand charge: recorded MD (kVA) × rate per kVA, minimum chargeable demand
Power factor adjustment: +penalty if avg PF < threshold, -incentive if PF > incentive threshold
FPPC surcharge: current quarter rate × total units consumed
Net metering credit: (export kWh × FIT rate) deducted from gross import charge
Arrears carry-forward: sum of balance_amount on OVERDUE/PARTIALLY_PAID previous bills
Late fee: 2% per month on arrears (simple interest, per month or part thereof)

Billing
APScheduler: 8th of month at 6am IST — batch all active contracts for current period
Parallel processing: group by MRU, run each group as an async task
Dry-run mode: compute bills without persisting — for operator preview before go-live
Error isolation: one failed bill does not abort the batch — errors logged separately
Post-run report: total bills generated, total amount, errors, estimated read %
Re-run capability: re-bill specific installations for a period (after reading correction)

MD Billing (Maximum Demand)
Billing
MD record creation: auto-populate from AMI interval max demand OR field entry
Billable MD = max(recorded_md, 75% of contract demand) — SERC minimum rule
MD charge line item on bill: distinct from energy charges on HT bill
MD comparison chart: last 12 months recorded vs contract demand — on PDF bill
Ratchet clause: if MD > contract demand → ratchet charge on excess
📄
PF Billing (Power Factor)
Billing
Average PF computation from interval kVAh and kWh data per billing period
Penalty slab: for every 0.01 below threshold PF → X% penalty on energy charge
Incentive slab: for every 0.01 above incentive threshold → X% rebate on energy
Maximum penalty cap: cannot exceed N% of base energy charge (configurable)
PF adjustment shown as signed line item on bill: positive=penalty, negative=incentive
📄
Net Metering (Solar Export)
Billing
POD flagged is_net_metered = TRUE with solar_capacity_kw recorded
Import reading and export reading from bi-directional meter or smart meter channels
Net units = import_kwh - export_kwh; if negative → credit carry-forward to next month
Export credit = export_kwh × FIT rate (defined by SERC, e.g. ₹3.00/kWh)
Annual settlement: remaining credit → NEFT refund or adjustment per SERC rules
